About Justin

I am a 4th year electrical engineering major at UC Davis focusing on physical design and VLSI. I have been working on many projects to deepen my understanding of physical design. I have been taking advantage of open-source tools like OpenLane2, as well as utilizing my school's computer lab to learn Synopsys' Design Compiler and their suite of IC design tools. I have been studying the fantastic Digital VLSI Design (RTL to GDS) lectures posted by Professor Adam Teman, and implementing the techniques he describes in my designs.


During my time interning at Lotus Communication Systems for two years, I have gained invaluable professional and communication skills. Working on complex projects with a team of 6-7 people highlighted the importance of effective communication and teamwork to me. As a result, the docmentation I wrote supporting my designs and reports I presented prioritized a succinct structure that clearly conveyed my message and the graphs and data I included were portrayed in a easy to comprehend manner.


In my four years at UC Davis, I have taken many electrical engineering classes. I have prioritized taking classes in the digital realm-particularly ones that involve circuit/physical design. However, I tried to select a wide range of classes that I thought would give me a holistic understanding of physical design. As a result, I believe the following list of classes I have selected reflects my priorities:

  • EEC 116 - VLSI Design (planned)
  • EEC 118 - Digital IC Design
  • EEC 170 - Computer Architecture
  • EEC 180 - Digital Systems II
  • EEC 216 - Low Power Digital IC Design (planned)
  • EEC 283 - Advanced Digital Verification Techniques
  • EEC 289O - IC Design and Tapeout (planned)
  • EEC 289Q - Deep Learning Hardware Design (planned)
